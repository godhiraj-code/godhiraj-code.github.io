--- title: "For those who haven't heard of Golang yet!" published: true ---
This was my first gopher conference , I reached before time and the first person I met was Mr Baiju. I have read his blogs on golang and it was great to interact with him.
The environment was amazing , few of the words you hear seems familiar and most of them were not. so that was a wake up call for me. There was still time for the conference to start so had the opportunity to talk to other participants .
As it turns out folks are using golang in various areas/companies/startups/collegekids. Tesco , justtickets , aerospike , digital ocean ,influx db ,etc .

so already met some awesome folks , now it was the time for the speakers .

The opening key note was by Brad Fitzpatrick, he started with history of golang , where the language stands now and what's the future if any. I will recommend to view the presentation talks once it is uploaded in youtube.
However, what I understood was that , go language is almost done (wrt language design) . The core team is kind of regretful that they could have removed some features from GO 1 , however if something is added in a language, it's very hard to remove that later.
Their idea is to provide the developers an extremely strong base where developers can code without any worry and there should not be any overhead associated with .

Then the talks were on the topic Interfaces . Aditya and Aaron gave the talk . I like perspective that Aditya said about interface -" Interface should be humbled and disciplined" .
He showed how error interface and reader/writer interface are powerful and how we can use it to our benefit. Please have a look at the presentation slides or videos of both.

There was a talk on go gaming . I was not impressed with the talk , it was not detailed and the speaker didn't really mentioned on how he used go features to develop the game.

Hana and Veronica gave talk on go mobile. The talks again were kind of repetitive . Hana demonstrated and Android app and Veronica was discussing on how she used go in her company , her talk was more on x vs y , why x and why y , why not x and why not y . Again it could have been more on golang and not compare it with others .
Had a conversation with Hana Kim , and it seems that we have to keep a watch on golang/mobile in upcoming time as things really look promising . 

It is turning out to be a very long blogpost so ending it here.
In short, It was an awesome gopher conference , speakers were wonderful both in Day one and Day Two.
